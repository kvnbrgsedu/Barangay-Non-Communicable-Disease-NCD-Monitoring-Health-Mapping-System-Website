Understanding Non-Communicable Diseases

Learn about common NCDs in the community, how they affect residents, and what can be done to prevent them.

Non-communicable diseases are long-term, chronic conditions that are not passed from person to person. They often develop slowly over time and are strongly linked to lifestyle, environment, and genetic factors.

Examples include hypertension, diabetes, cardiovascular diseases, and chronic respiratory conditions. NCDs are now among the leading causes of illness and death worldwide and in local communities.

  • Hypertension – persistently high blood pressure that can damage the heart, brain, and kidneys over time.
  • Diabetes – a condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar levels.
  • Cardiovascular Diseases – conditions affecting the heart and blood vessels, such as heart attack and stroke.
  • Chronic Respiratory Diseases – long-term lung conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).

Regular Exercise

Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate physical activity most days of the week to keep your heart and body strong.

Healthy Diet

Choose more vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and lean proteins. Limit salty, sugary, and fatty foods.

Regular Checkups

Visit your health center for blood pressure, blood sugar, and cholesterol screening, especially if you are at risk.

Avoid Smoking & Excessive Alcohol

Quitting smoking and moderating alcohol intake greatly reduces the risk of heart and lung diseases.

Your barangay health center is your first point of contact for NCD screening, counseling, and follow-up care.

  • Barangay Health Center – for consultations, vital sign checks, and medication refills.
  • Barangay Health Workers (BHWs) – for home visits, monitoring, and education.
  • Referral Hospitals & Clinics – for specialized laboratory tests and treatment.
